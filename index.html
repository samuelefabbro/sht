What does "Italian food" really mean?

To Italian people, not much! That's because every single region and every single city has its own typical dishes, ingredients, and types of pasta. We're not taking you to eat “amatriciana” in Venice (because it was invented in Amatrice!), we want you to experience the real thing. Ready?

Baccalà Mantecato

You can really judge a restaurant by trying its Baccalà. It’s a codfish dish that's boiled and mixed only with sunflower oil. Creamy and yummy, it's served on toasted bread or - even better - polenta corn.

Pasta e Fasioi
(with beans)

We love it! It's rustic, comforting and oh so satisfying. The beans are boiled together with bay leaf, rosemary and ham. For pasta, it's served with tagliatelle or ditalini and sprinkled parmesan.

Tirame-Su

It’s a must-have dessert in Venice, if not all of Italy, and the perfect ending to a delicious meal.
Enjoy ladyfingers soaked in coffee and covered with yolk cream and mascarpone.
Tirame-su means "lift me up." You’ll see why!

FAQs

1 - Are food and drinks included?

Yes, of course! There will be plenty to eat and drink. We'll make a minimum of 5 stops, with food and drinks available at each. If you don't drink alcohol, we'll find alternatives.

2 - Is there a min. and max. number of guests?

The tour will have a minimum of 2 guests and a maximum of 10. If the tour's full, send us an email. We'll see if we can make room for more.

3 - Is the payment transaction secure?

The payment system is handled by Stripe and PayPal, and your card details won't be stored in any way.

4 - Is it possible to cancel the tour?

The tickets are paid in advance. You can cancel for a refund up to 7 days before the tour, but no later. Send us an email if you have any extenuating circumstances.

5 - What language will the tour be in?

English.

6 - Can I shop during the tour?

No. But wine bottles will be available for purchase at some of the stops.

7 - What if the weather's bad?

The tour will go on. Bring a raincoat, boots and umbrella. In case of extraordinary weather, we'll let you know in advance and issue a full refund.

8 - Where and when does the tour start and end?

The tour starts in San Giacometto Square, next to the Rialto Bridge at 5.00 pm. It ends in San Bartolomeo Square at 8.30 pm. You'll get the full details in your email confirmation.

9 - I have dietary restrictions, can I still join?

Send us an email and we'll see what we can do. The tour covers Venetian specialties, which are generally fish- and meat-based.

10 - What about kids?

Kids are more than welcome, and get in free up to 10 yrs old.

11 - What about gratuities to the guides?

Tips are appreciated but entirely at your discretion.
